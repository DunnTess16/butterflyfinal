Southern Dogface

Zerene Cesonia

❮ ❯

Family:

Pieridae

Identification:

Upperside forewings of both sexes have yellow "dog's head" surrounded by black. "Eye" does not touch black border. Female similar, with duller, more diffuse black areas and 2 seasonal forms: underside hindwing of wet season "summer" form is yellow; that of dry season "winter" form is mottled with black and pink.

Habitat:

Dry, open areas such as short-grass prairie hills, scrub oak groves, open woodland, washes, road edges.

Flight:

Three flights in the southern states from May-June, July-August, and September-April. Northern colonists have one generation per year, or do not reproduce at all.

Adult Food:

Nectar from flowers including alfalfa, coreopsis, houstonia, and verbena.
