Mead's sulphur

Colias meadii

❮ ❯

Family:

Pieridae

Identification:

Upperside of male wings deep reddish orange with wide, dull black border; female lighter orange, border containing orange spots. Underside of both sexes greenish-yellow, with hindwing white spot circled in red.

Habitat:

At or near treeline in high mountains, tundra, subalpine meadows.

Flight:

One flight from July-August.

Adult Food:

Flower nectar including alpine sunflower and asters.
