Variegated fritillary

Euptoieta claudia

❮ ❯

Family:

Nymphalidae

Identification:

Upperside of wings are a tawny orange with thick dark veins and markings; black spots near margin. Hindwing margin angled and slightly scalloped. Underside of hindwing with a mottled pattern and no silver spots.

Habitat:

Open sunny areas such as prairies, fields, pastures, road edges, landfills.

Flight:

Three broods from April-October in the north, four broods from February or March-November or December in the south.

Adult Food:

Nectar from several plant species including butterflyweed, common milkweed, dogbane, peppermint, red clover, swamp milkweed, and tickseed sunflower.
