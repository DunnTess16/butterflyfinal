Silvery Blue

Glaucopsyche lygdamus

❮ ❯

Family:

Polyommatinae

Identification:

Upperside of male iridescent silvery blue with narrow dark borders; female darker blue with wide borders. Both sexes have white fringe. Underside gray-brown; both wings with row of white-ringed, round black spots. Subspecies xerces (Boisduval) and pseudoxerces Emmel and Emmel have large white spots with or without black centers.

Habitat:

A variety of locations including open woods, coastal dunes, prairies, meadows, road edges, rocky moist woods, and brushy fields.

Flight:

One flight from March-June at low elevations, June-August at high elevations.

Adult Food:

Nectar from flowers including Asteraceae.
