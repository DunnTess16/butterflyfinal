Atala Butterfly

Eumaeus atala

❮ ❯

Family:

Lycaenidae

Identification:

Its abdomen is a red-orange. Upperside of male wings soft black with iridescent green overlay and markings; female has blue iridescence at basal areas. Underside of wings dull black, hindwing with large red-orange spot and 3 rows of irregular iridescent gold spots.

Habitat:

Subtropical shady hammocks and neighboring open areas; gardens with ornamental cycads.

Flight:

Many flights throughout the year, but most common in early summer.

Adult Food:

Nectar from flowers including lantana, periwinkle, shepherd's needle, and wild coffee.
