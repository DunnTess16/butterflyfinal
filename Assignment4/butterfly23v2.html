Edith's checkerspot

Euphydryas editha

❮ ❯

Family:

Nymphalidae

Identification:

Variable. Front wing rounded at tip. Upperside is black with red and pale yellow or white bands. Submarginal band of small yellow or white spots; wide postmedian band is red. Underside of hindwing with alternating bands of yellow-orange and cream.

Habitat:

From the sea level to treeline through diverse habitats including coastal chaparral, meadows, fields, foothills, open woods, alpine fellfields.

Flight:

One brood; from March-May at low elevations, June-August at high elevations and in the north.

Adult Food:

Flower nectar.
