Butterflies of North America.

Southern Dogface, Zerene cesonia

A resident in South America north to southern Texas and peninsular Florida. Also a stray or temporary resident from central California northeast through the Great Lakes area and all the eastern states.

Garita Skipper, Oarisma garita

Lives in the high plains from central Alberta east to southern Manitoba, south through the Rocky Mountain region to northern Mexico. Strays east to central Ontario.

White Checkered, Pontia protodice

A permanent resident in southern United States and northern Mexico; temporary in northern United States and southern Canada.

Silvery Blue, Glaucopsyche lygdamus

Seen in central Alaska south to southern California, Baja California, Arizona, New Mexico, and western Kansas. Also along northern United States east to Nova Scotia and south to Georgia.

Viceroy, Limenitis archippus

A resident of the Northwest Territories south along the eastern edges of the Cascade and Sierra Nevada mountains to central Mexico, east through all the eastern United States.

Eyed Brown, Satyrodes eurydice

Most seen in Eastern North America from Nova Scotia south to Delaware and west to Saskatchewan and eastern Nebraska. Outlying populations in north-central Colorado, east-central Alberta, and northern Quebec.

Milbert's Tortoiseshell, Aglais milberti

Seen in Boreal North America south of the taiga. Southern Alaska south to California, Nevada, and New Mexico; east to Newfoundland and West Virginia.

Canadian Tiger swallowtail, Papilio canadensis

Found in North America from central Alaska southeast across Canada and the northern Great Lakes states to northern New England.

Monarch Butterfly, Danaus plexippus

A resident of Southern Canada south through all of the United States, Central America, and most of South America. Also present in Australia, Hawaii, and other Pacific Islands.

Black Swallowtail, Papilio polyxenes

Seen in most of the eastern U.S., north into Quebec, west into s. Saskatchewan, Colorado and se. California; south to n. South America. Subspecies coloro in desert Southwest.

Northern Crescent, Phyciodes cocyta

A resident of Newfoundland and northern New England west across the Great Lake states and southern Canada to British Columbia; south in the western mountains to Utah, southeast Arizona, and southern New Mexico; south in the Appalachians to Pennsylvania, Virginia, and West Virginia.

Weidemeyer's Admiral, Limenitis weidemeyerii

Seen in southern Alberta south to Nebraska and east-central California, southeastern Arizona and southern New Mexico.

Question Mark, Polygonia interrogationis

A resident of southern Canada and all of the eastern United States except peninsular Florida, west to the eastern edge of the Rocky Mountains, south to southern Arizona and Mexico.

Variegated Fritillary, Euptoieta claudia

Seen in higher elevations of Argentina through Central America and Mexico to the southern United States; also Cuba and Jamaica. Regularly colonizes north through most of the United States except the Pacific Northwest.

Lorquin's Admiral, Limenitis lorquini

British Columbia south to southern California and Baja California, east to western Montana and Idaho.

Edith's Checkerspot, Euphydryas editha

Resident of southern British Columbia and Alberta south to Baja California, Nevada, Utah, and Colorado.

Mead's Sulphur, Colias meadii

Restricted to the Rocky Mountains from Alberta south to Colorado.

Northwestern Fritillary, Speyeria hesperis

Resident in the Western mountains from central western Alaska, Yukon, and Northwest Territories south to central California, Arizona, and New Mexico.

American Snout, Libytheana carinenta

Seen in Argentina north through Mexico and the West Indies to southern United States. Migrates to central California, southern Nevada, Colorado, and most of the eastern United States.

Rocky Mountain Parnassian, Parnassius smintheusa

From New Mexico north along the Rocky Mountains and into southwest Alaska.

Mourning Cloak, Nymphalis antiopa

All of North America south of the tundra to central Mexico; rarely in the Gulf States and peninsular Florida. Also native to temperate Eurasia.

Pipevine Swallowtail, Battus philenor

Rarely strays to Canada (s. Manitoba). Seen mostly in the tropical lowlands south to southern Mexico.

Atala Butterfly, Eumaeus atala

Resident of southeastern Florida and the Keys, the Bahamas, and Cuba.
