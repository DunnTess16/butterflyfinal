White Checkered

Zerene cesonia

❮ ❯

Family:

Pieridae

Identification:

Upperside of male forewing has black checkered pattern on outer half. Female is more heavily patterned, but markings are more brown and diffuse. Hindwing of both male and female white. Underside of male hindwing with pale checkered pattern. Underside of female with yellow-tan markings on hindwing and tip of forewing. Short-day form has underside hindwing veins with heavy gray-green outlines.

Habitat:

Wide variety of sites including dry weedy areas, vacant lots, fields, pastures, sandy areas, railroad beds, and roads.

Flight:

Three flights, with a partial 4th in the South, from March-November.

Adult Food:

Flower nectar including hedge mustards, composites, and alfalfa.
