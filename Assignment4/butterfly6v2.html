Brown Eyed

Satyrodes eurydice

❮ ❯

Family:

Nymphalidae

Identification:

Upperside of wings are light to medium brown with black eyespots. Underside of forewing has submarginal eyespots about the same size, usually touching or linked like a chain; dark line inside the hindwing's spot row is zigzagged.

Habitat:

Open sedge meadows, freshwater marshes, slow-moving streams, cord grass swales in tallgrass prairie.

Flight:

One brood from June-September.

Adult Food:

Sap, bird droppings, and occasionally flower nectar.
