Northern Cresent

Phyciodes cocyta

❮ ❯

Family:

Nymphalidae

Identification:

Antennal clubs of male are orange. Females are darker than males. Upperside is orange-brown with dark borders; median orange-brown areas are mostly open, with few dark markings. Underside of hindwing is orange with a tan patch surrounding the pale marginal crescent.

Habitat:

Moist open areas in rocky places, wooded streams, marsh edges, and shale barrens.

Flight:

One brood from June-July, perhaps two broods in southern Canada.

Adult Food:

Nectar from flowers of dogbane, fleabane, and white clover.
